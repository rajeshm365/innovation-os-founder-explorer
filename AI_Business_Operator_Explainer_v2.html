How the AI Business Operator Actually Works

A plain-English walkthrough of the pilot system — what each piece does, why it exists, and how they talk to each other. No computer science degree needed.

The Problem

A small business in India — say, a clinic or a salon — may miss calls when the owner is busy with a customer. Every missed call is a potential missed booking. We're building an AI receptionist that can answer those calls, chat with the caller, and book appointments — with human oversight for exceptions.

Pilot Reality Check

This is a pilot system for one customer. The goal is learning, not perfection. The AI handles routine booking calls. When it gets stuck, it transfers to a real person. We monitor every call and improve the system daily based on what we find.

The Phone Call Journey

Here's what happens from the moment someone dials the business number. Six phases. The typical call takes 2–4 minutes.

1
Exotel
The call lands on an Indian phone number

The business gets a real Indian phone number — something like 080-XXXX-XXXX. When a customer dials it, a service called Exotel catches it. Exotel is India-focused, with INR billing and regulatory support for Indian virtual numbers. Its only job is to pick up the call and pass it to our AI system via a SIP trunk (a digital phone line).

Analogy

Exotel is the front door. It doesn't do anything clever — it opens the door and lets the caller in.

2
Retell AI + Supabase
The system checks if this caller has called before

Before the AI says a word, it checks the caller's phone number against our database (Supabase — a cloud database). Has this person called before? What's their name? The context is loaded and given to the AI so it can personalize the greeting.

Important: The AI doesn't assume who's calling — it confirms: "I believe we've spoken before — is this Priya?" because family members may share a phone.

Analogy

Like a receptionist who checks the visitor log before saying hello — but still asks "is that you?" rather than assuming.

3
Retell AI (Deepgram + GPT-4o + Cartesia inside)
The conversation happens — turn by turn

This is the core loop. Every time the caller speaks, three things happen in sequence — all managed by Retell AI:

Listen → Think → Speak.

Deepgram converts the caller's voice to text in real time. It's the ears. It supports Indian English (en-IN locale), though real-world accuracy needs testing with actual callers.

GPT-4o reads that text and decides what to say back. It knows the business's services, hours, and rules from a system prompt we configure. It's the brain.

Cartesia turns GPT-4o's text into a natural-sounding voice and plays it to the caller. It's the mouth. Cartesia is designed for low-latency voice (vendor-stated sub-90ms time-to-first-audio).

Retell manages all three internally — we don't handle audio streams ourselves. We just configure which providers to use in Retell's dashboard.

Analogy

Deepgram = ears. GPT-4o = brain. Cartesia = mouth. Retell = the nervous system connecting all three. We configure the nervous system; it handles the wiring.

4
GPT-4o → Retell → FastAPI → Google Calendar
The system confirms availability and books the appointment

When GPT-4o decides it's time to book, it sends a signal (a "function call intent"). It doesn't book anything itself — it has no internet access. Retell intercepts this signal and calls our FastAPI server.

FastAPI is a small Python program (~250 lines) that acts as the AI's hands. Here's what it does, in order:

1. Verify. It checks Google Calendar to confirm the slot is actually free. It never trusts the AI's assumption — the slot might have been taken by a walk-in since the conversation started.

2. Book. If the slot is free, FastAPI creates the Calendar event.

3. Confirm. Only after the event is actually created does FastAPI tell the AI "confirmed." The AI then tells the caller. If anything fails (timeout, API error), FastAPI tells the AI "failed" — and the AI offers to take the caller's number for a callback instead of lying about a booking.

Analogy

GPT-4o is the manager who decides what to do. FastAPI is the assistant who checks the book and writes the appointment in ink. The manager doesn't write — they say "book it," and the assistant verifies before writing.

5
Retell → Founder's Phone
If the AI gets stuck, it transfers to a real person

This is the safety net. If the caller asks to speak to a human, or the AI can't understand after two tries, or the caller sounds frustrated — the system transfers the call to a founder's mobile phone. The AI says: "Let me connect you with someone who can help."

Every transfer is logged with the reason why. This tells us exactly where the AI is failing, so we can fix the system prompt and reduce future transfers.

Analogy

Like a trainee receptionist who knows when to say "let me get the manager." A good trainee doesn't pretend to know everything — they escalate early and learn from each handoff.

6
FastAPI → MSG91 + Slack + Supabase
The caller hangs up — the machine keeps working

After the call ends, three things happen automatically. The caller gets an SMS confirmation via MSG91. The founders get a Slack notification with the call summary (who called, what happened, was an appointment booked). The full call record gets saved to Supabase.

None of this waits for a human to press a button. And none of it affects the caller — they've already hung up.

The Tools, Plain and Simple

Here's every tool in the stack. One card each.

Exotel
Phone Number Provider

Gives the business a real Indian phone number and routes incoming calls to our AI. India-focused, INR billing, documented integration with Retell AI.

Retell AI
Voice Pipeline Manager

The command centre. Connects the phone call to the AI brain, manages the conversation, and calls our code when the AI needs to act. Handles Deepgram, GPT-4o, and Cartesia internally.

Deepgram Nova-3
Ears (Speech to Text)

Converts spoken words into text in real time. Supports Indian English (en-IN). Accuracy on real Indian phone audio to be validated in week 1 testing.

GPT-4o
Brain (AI Decision-Maker)

Reads the conversation, decides what to say, and knows when to book an appointment, ask another question, or transfer to a human.

Cartesia Sonic
Mouth (Text to Speech)

Converts GPT-4o's text into natural-sounding voice. Designed for low latency so the conversation feels responsive.

FastAPI
The AI's Hands

A small Python server (~250 lines) that checks calendars, creates bookings, sends SMS, and posts alerts. The AI decides; FastAPI verifies and acts.

Google Calendar
Appointment Store

Where bookings live. FastAPI checks it for available slots and creates events. If the pilot customer doesn't use one, we set it up during onboarding.

Supabase
Memory + Logs

Stores caller history, past bookings, and call logs. Gives the AI memory across calls. Free tier is sufficient for the pilot.

MSG91
SMS Confirmations

Sends SMS booking confirmations to callers. India-focused, works immediately, no approval process needed.

Railway.app
Hosting

Where our FastAPI server lives on the internet. Push code, it's deployed. Estimated cost ~₹336/month.

Slack
Founder Alerts

Every call, booking, transfer, and error gets posted to a Slack channel. Founders see what's happening without logging into any dashboard.

Human Transfer
Safety Net

Built into Retell. When the AI is stuck or the caller asks for a person, the call transfers to a founder's phone. Every transfer is logged so we learn from it.

What Does It Cost?

A human receptionist in India may cost roughly ₹15,000–25,000 per month, typically with fixed working hours. This system can operate outside business hours and handle multiple callers, subject to API uptime. At ~₹4,440/month infrastructure cost, if the service is priced at ₹7,000–10,000/month, the gross margin could be positive — but actual profitability depends on call volume, duration, and support overhead. These numbers need validation with a real customer.

Cost Safeguard

Maximum call duration is capped at 8 minutes in Retell. If monthly minutes exceed 600, founders get an automatic Slack alert to investigate. Free credits from Deepgram ($200) and Retell ($10) reduce Month 1 out-of-pocket cost to approximately ₹2,500–3,000.

Four Things to Remember

  • The AI never books without verification. FastAPI checks Google Calendar before confirming any appointment. If the Calendar API fails, the AI tells the caller it couldn't confirm and offers a callback — it never lies about a booking.
  • When the AI gets stuck, it transfers to a person. There is always a human fallback. The caller never gets trapped in a loop. Every transfer is logged so we learn where the AI needs to improve.
  • Deepgram and Cartesia are Retell's internal tools. We don't manage audio streams. We pick the providers in Retell's dashboard and it handles the wiring.
  • The pilot is about learning, not perfection. We monitor every call, improve the system prompt daily, and make a kill/continue decision weekly based on real data.
The 10-Second Pitch

Someone calls. Exotel picks up. Retell runs the conversation. Deepgram listens. GPT-4o thinks. Cartesia speaks. FastAPI verifies and books. MSG91 confirms. Slack notifies. If anything goes wrong, the call transfers to a founder's phone. The business owner checks their calendar and sees bookings they didn't have to answer the phone for.

Week 1 Goal

10 test calls. Measure: Can Deepgram understand Indian English callers? Is the response time under 2 seconds? Can the booking flow complete end-to-end? Does the transfer work? If all four pass, we deploy to the pilot customer. If any fail, we fix or kill.
